Vocabulary
A working glossary

The language of this calendar

These terms are orientations. Ways of entering a different relationship with time and continuity.

Core concepts
Drift
The growing distance between a practice and the last act that renewed it. Drift is information: a record of what has not been returned to, and for how long. Gregorian calendars measure time passing. This calendar measures drift accumulating.
Return
An act of renewal that restores continuity. Return is the decision to carry something forward, which may or may not involve recovering what was there before. When enough practices in a field are returned to, the calendar moves. Time advances because a community has acted.
Release
A conscious decision to end something rather than let it disappear by accident. Drift is abandonment without decision. Release is the community choosing to close something with care, which is why the calendar moves when something is released: ambiguity has ended.
Rehearsal
The practice of return before return is needed. Rehearsal keeps the capacity alive: the muscle memory of gathering, the knowledge of who holds what, the social infrastructure of showing up. A community that rehearses its practices does not need to rebuild them from scratch when crisis arrives.
Cycle types

Each practice moves on its own temporal logic. These are the reasons a community returns, not durations.

Breath
Return here is showing up on the night, picking up an instrument, sitting in the room. The practice is the gathering itself. When the gathering stops, the cycle breaks immediately and the absence is felt in the body before it is named.
Neighbourhood Jazz Night
Covenant
Return here is the act of choosing again: walking through the door, sitting down, keeping the shared habit alive by doing it. The thing exists only while people keep choosing it. Thins by non-decision: no one announces the ending, it simply stops being chosen.
Public Library Reading Room
Friction
Return here is the meeting itself: neighbours sitting down to repair a shared rule before it becomes a grievance. The practice does not run on a schedule. It runs on the pressure that builds when people share space and stop talking about it.
Apartment Courtyard Assembly
Vigil
Return here is the act of checking: reading the output, running the review, asking whether the code still does what it claims. The infrastructure runs without the watching, but the watching is what keeps it trustworthy. When no one looks, nothing breaks immediately. Something is simply no longer being held.
Open Source Security Circle
Transmission
Return here is the session: one person showing another how to graft a branch, how to open a radio chassis, how to hold the tool. The cycle is measured in who remains willing to teach. When the last person who knows moves away or dies, the cycle breaks permanently and no amount of documentation recovers it.
Old Orchard Grafting Circle / Community Radio Repair Day
Threshold
Return here is the review: someone deciding that the city has changed enough to ask again whether this route still serves the people on it. The practice does not run on a date. It runs when someone takes responsibility for noticing the gap between what the infrastructure promises and what the people using it experience.
Night Bus Route Review
Listening
Return here is going to the river: reading the water level, noting the colour, asking what has changed since last time. The river is not a resource being monitored. It is a subject being listened to. The practice is the act of showing up with attention rather than instruments.
River Monitoring Program
Ecological
Return here is the exchange itself: seeds passed between hands after harvest, before planting. The date is not set in advance. The season sets it. When the harvest comes, the exchange happens. When the harvest is missed, a season of diversity is lost.
Seed Exchange
Climatic
Return here is opening the shelter before the heat arrives: checking the water supply, telling the neighbourhood it is open, making sure someone is there. The heat season is the window, roughly three months. Missing it is not a scheduling failure. It is a preparedness failure, and people pay for it with their lives.
Heat Shelter Network
Reckoning
Return here is riding the route and asking the people on it: does this train still go where you need to go? Does it run when you need it to run? The practice is the act of confronting the gap between what the infrastructure was built for and what it is actually doing for the people whose lives depend on it.
Rural Night Train Review
Memorial
Return here is cooking together: who brings what, who stands where, what gets made, what gets passed around. The knowledge is not in a recipe. It is in the doing alongside each other. The cycle returns twice a year because memory needs rehearsing before it needs recovering. When the people who remember are gone, what remains is only the form.
Festival Kitchen Rota
